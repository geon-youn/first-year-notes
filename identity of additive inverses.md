# Identity of Additive Inverses
From [[vector space]], we claim that $(-\vec{u})=(-1)\vec{u}$.

```ad-abstract
title: Proof
$$
\begin{align*}
\vec{u}+(-1)\vec{u}=(1+(-1))\vec{u}=0\vec{u}=0	
\end{align*}
$$
So, $(-1)\vec{u}$ is the **unique** inverse of $\vec{u}$, so it must be the same as $-\vec{u}$.  
```
